What is personal data?

Personal data means information relating to a natural person who can be identified from that data, whether directly or indirectly. That identification can be by the information alone, or by the information and any other information that is in the possession of the data controller or likely to come into the data controller’s possession. Consequently, the definition of personal data is wide.

The processing of personal data is controlled by the GDPR.

What information does SmartexLab collect about you?

SmartexLab collects two types of information about you.

1: ID and contact information. First and last names, title, email address, phone number.

2: Automatically collected technical information. Information acquired using Google Analytics and any other website management and optimization tools we may use. These record visitor behaviors and help us optimize our Website for you. You can learn more about Google Analytics here: https://support.google.com/analytics/answer/1012034?hl=en&ref_topic=6157800.

We do not collect any other types of data. We do not collect any kind of special personal data, defined by GDPR as data including details of your race or ethnicity, religious or philosophical beliefs, sex life, sexual orientation, political opinions, trade union membership, information about your health and genetic or biometric data.

Please contact us with any changes to your personal information and will take reasonable steps to update our records and ensure that the information about you that is in our possession is accurate and up-to-date.

What is SmartexLab’s cookie policy?

Most websites use cookies. SmartexLab’s website uses cookies to improve your user experience. A cookie is a small file stored on your computer or device when you visit a website, which serves to identify you to that website when you visit again. It can also be used to store your personal preferences with regard to that website, so it can serve you the best user experience possible.

When you visit our website for the first time, cookies are created and stored on your computer or device. The next time you visit, the cookie is sent back to us, identifying you to the Website. We don’t create cookies ourselves. Instead, we have third-party providers who do it for us.

Cookies make it easier for you to use our website when you visit. They let us monitor traffic and personalize the content of our website for you. SmartexLab uses several different types of cookies.

Session-based cookies

Session-based cookies last only while your browser is open. When you close your browser these cookies are automatically deleted from your computer or device.

Persistent cookies

Persistent cookies last until you delete them or until your browser deletes them, if it is set to delete cookies on a schedule, or until they automatically expire.

Functional cookies

Some of our cookies are necessary for certain services that we offer on the Website. For instance, some cookies enable basic functions on the Website like page navigation. The Website will not function properly without these cookies.

Analytics cookies

Analytics cookies collect information on how visitors use our Website, including which content and products users view most frequently and how effective our third-party advertisements are. Google Analytics uses this type of cookie to collect and analyze user behavior information so we can optimize our site.

This information includes which source users came to the site from (for instance, Google search), which IP address the visitor has and which country they’re in, which pages they open, when those pages were opened and how long that user stayed on those pages, and which browser, device, operating system and language a visitor was using. This data is anonymous and is used to improve the content and functionality of the website.

Components of Google Analytics are embedded into pages of the Website. Use of Google Analytics and Google Adwords is covered by the terms of the Google Privacy Policy which you can view at: https://policies.google.com/privacy?hl=en.

The Adroll services we use to serve advertiszing from third parties on our Website uses cookies to deliver more relevant ads across the web. Adroll components are also embedded into pages on the Website. Use of Adroll is covered by the terms of the Adroll privacy policy which you can view here: https://www.adrollgroup.com/privacy

Can you control your cookies?

You can control your own cookie settings, including refusing to accept any cookies and deleting all cookies after each site visit, though we do not recommend this. The functionality to do this is built into your browser and your mobile device.

To learn more about how to control your cookie settings through your browser, please select your browser from the list below:

  • Firefox
  • Chrome
  • Internet Explorer
  • Safari (Desktop)
  • Safari (Mobile)
  • Android Browser

For other browsers, please consult the documentation provided by your browser manufacturer.

Can you opt out of interest-based ad targeting?

You can opt out of interest-based advertisement targeting through the Digital Advertising Alliance.  See here: http://www.aboutads.info/ for more information.

Additionally, you can change the privacy settings on your iPhone, iPad or Android device to control whether or not you see interest-based advertisements.

iOS device

iOS 7 or Higher

Go to your Settings > Select Privacy > Select Advertising > Enable the “Limit Ad Tracking” setting

OS 6

Go to your Settings >Select General > Select About > Select Advertising > Enable the “Limit Ads Tracking” setting

More information about the iOS setting can be found here.

Android device

Open your Google Settings app > Ads > Enable “Opt out of interest-based advertising”
